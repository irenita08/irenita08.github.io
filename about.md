- I’m from a little town on the northern coast of Puerto Rico (now emerging from obscurity due to also being the hometown of Bad Bunny). This is still home, where my mom and brother and dog live and I always yearn to return. I never quite feel at home if I’m far from the ocean and the sun, except in Bologna, where I spent an idyllic year as a graduate student in my mid-twenties.

- My first and enduring intellectual love was and remains understanding “the rest of the world.” Maybe because I grew up in a small town in a small island and couldn’t travel much as a kid,  I always just wanted to *leave*, to see and understand as much of the world as possible. Even now I don’t do well with the idea of staying put in the same place indefinitely, so I recently left my job to travel for a while and learn things I didn't learn in formal schooling (I studied international relations, economics, and development at Penn and at Johns Hopkins SAIS, and finance via the CFA program).

- I speak Spanish, English, and semi-decent Italian. A long time ago I minored in Chinese language and history, but my surviving knowledge of Mandarin is pitiful since I haven't lived in China since college and never worked in Chinese.

- Until now (I’m 36), I’ve been a consultant, an emerging markets private equity investor, and a risk management expert in a few settings (a digital assets exchange and a global bank).

- While I learned a lot from each of those, I’m not yet sure what my career “noun” is or should be, which is the other big reason for quitting my job to take an exploratory year off.
  
- I've realized that I measure "success" and life satisfaction primarily by:
  - number of new countries / places explored in a given time period;
  - number of interesting books read and new things learned (via work or otherwise); and
  - (most importantly) time spent with Sage and my favorite humans.


- This sabbatical is an attempt to reorient my life around those things, and figure out what I’m meant to be doing with the precious little time we’re gifted on this planet.
